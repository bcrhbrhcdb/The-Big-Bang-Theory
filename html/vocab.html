Vocabulary

Redshift

The shifting of spectral lines toward longer wavelengths in radiation from far-off galaxies and astronomical objects, proportional to the recession velocity, is known as Doppler shift.

Blueshift

Astronomers use the term "blueshift," which describes the shift in light wavelengths toward the blue end of the spectrum, to comprehend how galaxies move toward us in space.

Singularity

A point of infinite density and gravity.

Cosmic Microwave Background

The energy emitted during the Big Bang as matter and antimatter destroyed one another is known as cosmic background radiation, or CBR. The CBR is a radiation that is almost uniform and comes from every part of the sky.

Hubble's Law

A law according to which the distance between galaxies determines the redshifts in their spectra and, consequently, their recessional speeds.
