Vocabulary

Redshift

The shifting of spectral lines toward longer wavelengths in radiation from far-off galaxies and astronomical objects, proportional to the recession velocity, is known as Doppler shift.

Blueshift

Astronomers use the term "blueshift," which describes the shift in light wavelengths toward the blue end of the spectrum, to comprehend how galaxies move toward us in space.
